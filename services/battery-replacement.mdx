## Signs You Need a Battery Replacement

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Rapid battery drain">
    Your iPhone used to last all day but now barely makes it to lunch. As battery capacity degrades, it simply holds less charge — meaning your phone runs out of power faster even with normal usage.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Unexpected shutdowns">
    Your iPhone powers off suddenly, even when the battery indicator shows 20%, 30%, or more remaining. This happens when the battery can no longer deliver stable power under load, causing the phone to shut itself down as a protective measure.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Battery health below 80%">
    Apple considers a battery to have reached the end of its designed lifespan when its maximum capacity falls below 80%. At this point, you'll likely experience noticeable performance and longevity issues in daily use.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Phone gets unusually hot">
    A degraded battery works harder to deliver the same output, generating excess heat in the process. If your iPhone regularly feels hot during normal tasks like browsing or streaming, the battery is a likely culprit.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Performance throttling">
    To protect an aging battery from causing unexpected shutdowns, iOS may automatically reduce your iPhone's processor speed. This can make the device feel sluggish — apps take longer to open, animations stutter, and the phone feels generally unresponsive.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## How to Check Your Battery Health

You can check your iPhone's current battery capacity directly in iOS:

1. Open the **Settings** app
2. Tap **Battery**
3. Tap **Battery Health & Charging**
4. Review the **Maximum Capacity** percentage

If the percentage is at or below **80%**, or if you see a message that your battery's "peak performance capability" is affected, it's time for a replacement.

<Info>
  Apple officially recommends replacing your iPhone battery when maximum capacity drops to 80% or below. At this threshold, many users experience noticeably reduced battery life and potential performance throttling under demanding workloads.
</Info>
