Water and electronics move fast in the wrong direction. The moment liquid enters your iPhone, corrosion begins — and every minute the device stays powered on accelerates the damage. Acting quickly gives your iPhone the best possible chance of recovery. At VoltVandal iPhone Lab, our water damage service uses professional-grade ultrasonic cleaning and component-level inspection to restore as much functionality as possible.

## What to Do Immediately

If your iPhone has been exposed to water, the next few minutes matter more than you might think. Follow these steps before anything else:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Power off your iPhone right away">
    Do not wait to see if it still works — turn it off immediately. Running electrical current through wet components dramatically increases the chance of a short circuit and permanent damage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Do not attempt to charge it">
    Plugging in a water-damaged iPhone is one of the most common ways people turn a recoverable device into an unrecoverable one. Keep it unplugged until a technician has inspected and cleared it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Skip the rice — bring it in instead">
    Despite the popular advice, leaving your phone in a bag of rice does little to remove corrosion and wastes critical recovery time. Get your device to us as soon as possible — the sooner we can clean it, the better the outcome.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove any case or accessories">
    Take off your case, remove your SIM card tray, and gently shake out any visible water from the ports. Do not use a hair dryer or compressed air, as heat and pressure can push moisture deeper into the device.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Our Water Damage Recovery Process

When you bring your iPhone in, here's what our recovery service involves:

- **Disassembly and visual inspection** — we open the device and assess the extent of liquid exposure, identifying which components have been affected
- **Ultrasonic cleaning** — the logic board and other affected components are placed in a professional ultrasonic cleaner, using a specialized solution to break apart and remove corrosion at a microscopic level
- **Thorough drying** — components are dried using controlled heat and airflow to eliminate all remaining moisture before reassembly
- **Component-level testing** — after cleaning, each major component (display, battery, cameras, charging port, speakers) is individually tested to determine what's functional
- **Reassembly and full device test** — once cleaned and dried, we reassemble the device and run a complete functional test

<Warning>
  Water damage recovery cannot be guaranteed. The outcome depends on how quickly the device was powered off, how long it was submerged, the type of liquid involved, and the extent of internal corrosion. There is a risk of partial or total data loss with any water-damaged device. We strongly recommend attempting a backup as soon as possible if your phone is still partially functional — but do not charge it to do so without consulting us first.
</Warning>

## iPhone Water Resistance: What IP Ratings Mean

Many modern iPhones carry an IP67 or IP68 water resistance rating, which means they can withstand limited water exposure under controlled lab conditions. However, water resistance is **not** permanent:

| Rating | Protection Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| IP67 | Up to 1 meter for 30 minutes | iPhone 8 through iPhone X |
| IP68 | Up to 6 meters for 30 minutes | iPhone 11 and later (depth varies by model) |

Water resistance degrades over time — especially if the device has been dropped, repaired, or subjected to repeated exposure. Salt water, pool water, and other liquids are far more corrosive than fresh water and can overwhelm even a highly rated seal. All iPhones we service, regardless of IP rating, are accepted for water damage recovery.
