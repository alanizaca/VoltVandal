  <Accordion title="iPhone screen is black or unresponsive">
    A black or frozen screen can be caused by a software crash, a drained battery, or a hardware issue. Work through these steps before assuming the worst.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Charge your iPhone for at least 20 minutes">
        Connect your iPhone to a known-working charger and cable. If the battery is fully depleted, it may take up to 20 minutes before the screen responds. Look for the low-battery charging icon to appear.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Perform a force restart">
        A force restart can clear a software freeze without erasing your data.
        - **iPhone 8 or later:** Press and quickly release Volume Up, press and quickly release Volume Down, then press and hold the Side button until the Apple logo appears.
        - **iPhone 7/7 Plus:** Press and hold both the Volume Down and Sleep/Wake buttons simultaneously until the Apple logo appears.
        - **iPhone 6s or earlier:** Press and hold both the Home and Sleep/Wake buttons simultaneously until the Apple logo appears.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Try a different charging cable and adapter">
        A faulty cable or adapter can prevent charging entirely, making it appear your screen is dead. Swap in a different cable and power brick to rule this out.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Check for physical display damage">
        Inspect your screen for cracks, pressure marks, or discoloration. A damaged display cable or broken LCD/OLED panel can cause a blank screen even when the phone is powered on.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Visit VoltVandal for a free diagnostic">
        If none of the above steps restore your display, bring your iPhone in. We'll diagnose whether it's a software issue, battery failure, or display hardware problem — at no charge.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Battery drains too fast">
    Rapid battery drain is one of the most common iPhone complaints. It's often caused by aging battery cells, power-hungry apps, or settings that need adjustment.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Check your battery health">
        Go to **Settings → Battery → Battery Health & Charging**. If your Maximum Capacity is below **80%**, your battery is significantly degraded and a replacement is recommended.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Review battery usage by app">
        Under **Settings → Battery**, scroll down to see which apps are consuming the most power. Unusually high usage from a single app may indicate it's misbehaving in the background.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Disable background app refresh for non-essential apps">
        Go to **Settings → General → Background App Refresh** and turn it off for apps that don't need to update in the background (social media, games, etc.).
      </Step>
      <Step title="Limit location services">
        Navigate to **Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services**. Set non-essential apps to "While Using" instead of "Always" to reduce constant GPS activity.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Turn on Low Power Mode temporarily">
        Enable **Settings → Battery → Low Power Mode** to extend your battery while you assess the issue. This reduces background activity, mail fetch, and visual effects.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Consider a battery replacement">
        If your battery health is below 80% or your phone can't hold a charge through a normal day, a professional battery replacement is the most effective solution. Most battery swaps at VoltVandal are completed same-day.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="iPhone won't charge">
    Charging failures are usually caused by a dirty port, a faulty cable, or a worn-out accessory — rather than a hardware defect inside the phone.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Inspect and clean the Lightning or USB-C port">
        Look into your charging port with a flashlight. Lint, dust, and debris are common culprits that prevent a solid connection. If you see buildup, carefully remove it using a **dry toothpick or soft brush** — never use metal objects or compressed air directly into the port.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Try a different certified cable and adapter">
        Use an Apple-certified (MFi) cable and a different power adapter. Non-certified cables can fail without warning. Try plugging directly into a wall outlet rather than a USB hub or computer port.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Test wireless charging (iPhone 8 and later)">
        If your iPhone supports wireless charging, place it on a Qi-compatible pad to determine whether the issue is isolated to the charging port or affects the entire charging system.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Force restart your iPhone">
        Occasionally a software glitch can prevent the charging system from responding. Perform a force restart (see the screen troubleshooting section above for model-specific steps) and then reconnect the charger.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Bring it in for a port inspection">
        If cleaning and cable swapping don't work, your charging port may be physically damaged or the charging IC on the logic board may need attention. Stop by for a free diagnostic — charging port replacements are among our fastest repairs.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Camera is blurry or shows a black screen">
    Camera problems can range from a smudged lens to a disconnected camera module. Start with the simplest fixes first.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Clean the camera lens">
        Use a **soft, lint-free microfiber cloth** to gently wipe the rear and front camera lenses. Fingerprints, oils, and dust are the most frequent cause of blurry photos and can be fixed in seconds.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Force close the Camera app">
        Swipe up from the bottom of the screen (or double-press Home on older models) to open the app switcher. Swipe the Camera app away to close it completely, then reopen it from the home screen.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Restart your iPhone">
        A standard restart can clear temporary software glitches affecting the camera system. Hold the Side button (and a Volume button on Face ID models) and slide to power off, then turn it back on.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Test both front and rear cameras">
        Open the Camera app and switch between the front (selfie) and rear cameras. If only one is affected, this points to a hardware issue with that specific camera module rather than a software problem.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Check for physical damage to the camera module">
        Inspect the camera bump for cracks or damage. A cracked lens cover or dropped-impact damage can permanently affect image quality and may require a camera module replacement.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Visit us for a camera diagnostic">
        Persistent black screens or autofocus failures that survive restarts typically indicate a hardware fault. Bring your iPhone in and we'll pinpoint the issue during your free diagnostic.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="iPhone got wet">
    Liquid damage is time-sensitive. The faster you act, the better your chances of saving your device. Follow these steps immediately.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Power off your iPhone immediately">
        Do not wait — turn your iPhone off right away. Press and hold the Side button (and a Volume button) and slide to power off. Keeping it on while wet risks short-circuiting the internal components.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Do not attempt to charge it">
        Plugging in a wet iPhone can cause serious short-circuit damage. Do not connect it to power, even if the phone appears to be working normally.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Remove the case and dry the exterior">
        Take off any case, remove your SIM card tray, and gently pat the exterior dry with a soft cloth or paper towel. Tilt the phone to let any water drain from the ports.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Do not use rice, heat guns, or hair dryers">
        The "rice trick" is a myth and can leave starch particles inside the device. Heat guns and hair dryers can warp internal components. Avoid these entirely.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Bring it to VoltVandal as soon as possible">
        Time is critical with liquid damage. The sooner our technicians can inspect, disassemble, and dry your device, the higher the chance of full recovery. We offer **same-day liquid damage assessments**. Bring it in even if the phone still seems to be working — hidden corrosion can develop over hours or days.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Touch screen not responding">
    An unresponsive or erratic touch screen can be caused by software glitches, screen protector interference, or a damaged digitizer. Try these steps first.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Remove your screen protector">
        Tempered glass protectors that are cracked, lifted at the edges, or misaligned can interfere with touch sensitivity. Remove the screen protector and test the screen directly to see if responsiveness improves.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Clean the screen surface">
        Moisture, grease, or debris on the screen can cause erratic touch behavior. Wipe the screen thoroughly with a dry microfiber cloth, and make sure your hands are clean and dry before testing.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Perform a force restart">
        A software crash can cause the touch layer to stop responding entirely. A force restart (see screen troubleshooting section for model-specific steps) will clear it without losing any data.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Check for physical screen damage">
        Cracks that pass through the display — especially near the corners — can damage the digitizer layer beneath the glass, causing dead zones where touch doesn't register. Look closely for hairline cracks you may have missed.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Update to the latest iOS version">
        Go to **Settings → General → Software Update**. Occasionally, iOS bugs affect touch input and are resolved in software updates. Make sure you're running the latest version.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Bring it in for a display assessment">
        If your screen has dead zones, ghost touches, or simply won't respond after trying the above steps, the digitizer or display assembly likely needs professional replacement. Stop by for a free diagnostic.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
